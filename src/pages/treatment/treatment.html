Rationale
To keep necrotic tissue dry, reduce the risk of wet gangrene and reduce the risk of infection. To hydrate necrotic tissue and aid debridement of necrotic tissue. To hydrate the slough and aid debridement of sloughy tissue. To hydrate slough and absorb exudate to aid the debridement of wet slough. To absorb exudate and promote a moist environment at the wound/ulcer surface. To prevent the wound/ulcer drying out and to promote epithelialisation. To act as an antimicrobial restoring bacterial balance to the wound/ulcer to promote healing. To reduce the biofilm to the wound/ulcer surface.
Bandage regime
Application of an orthopaedic wool and K lite bandage from toe to knee to retain dressings. Application of an orthopaedic wool and K lite bandage from toe to ankle to retain dressings. Application of an orthopaedic wool and two K lite bandages from toe to knee to retain dressings and offer a limited level of pressure to promote venous return and reduce oedema in patients who have peripheral arterial disease. Application of a 3-layer bandage consisting of an orthopaedic wool, K lite and K Plus bandage from toe to knee to acknowledge arterial disease, promote venous return and reduce oedema to the limb. Application of a 4-layer bandage consisting of an orthopaedic wool, K lite, K Plus and Ko Flex bandage from toe to knee to promote venous return. As the ankle circumference is greater than 25 cm apply a 4-layer bandage consisting of an orthopaedic wool, K Plus, K Plus and Ko Flex bandage from toe to knee to promote venous return. Application of a K Two bandage from toe to knee to promote venous return. Application of a K Two light bandage from toe to knee to acknowledge arterial disease, promote venous return and reduce oedema to the limb. Application of an Activa 40 mmHg Leg Ulcer Hosiery Kit to promote venous return Application of a hosiery liner to offer 10 mmHg of reduced compression at the ankle to acknowledge arterial disease, promote venous return and reduce oedema to the limb. Application of a farrow wrap to promote venous return and reduce oedema. Application of a Juxta Cures wrap to promote venous return and reduce oedema. Use of a K Lite bandage to retain dressings.
Frequency of dressings
Change dressings daily to observe and monitor the wound/ulcer. Change alternate days Change three times a week Change twice weekly Change weekly Change alternate days initially but assess the level of wound/ulcer exudate to dictate the exact timescale of dressing changes.
